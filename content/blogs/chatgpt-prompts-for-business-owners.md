Business owners do not need more random prompt lists. They need prompts connected to real business outcomes: clearer communication, faster follow-up, better offers, stronger processes, and fewer repeated mistakes.

The prompts below are designed for everyday business work. Adapt them to your context, check the output, and save the versions that work for your business.

For a complete business-focused learning path, see [AI for Everyday Business Owners](/courses/ai-for-everyday-business-owners/).

## Before using any prompt

Give ChatGPT the details that matter:

- what your business does
- who your customer is
- what the customer wants
- what your policy actually says
- what tone you want
- what the next step should be

A prompt without context will produce generic output. A prompt with context can produce usable work.

## Customer enquiry prompts

Prompt:

"Write a clear reply to a customer asking about [question]. My business is [business type]. Our answer is [actual answer]. Include the next step. Keep it short enough for WhatsApp."

Prompt:

"Create three versions of this reply: friendly, premium, and very brief. Do not change the facts: [paste reply]."

Prompt:

"Rewrite this message so it sounds helpful but firm. The customer is asking for [request], but our policy is [policy]: [paste draft]."

These prompts help you respond faster without weakening your standards.

## Sales and offer prompts

Prompt:

"Review this offer and identify what is unclear, what objections a customer may have, and what details should be added before publishing: [paste offer]."

Prompt:

"Rewrite this offer so it clearly explains who it is for, what is included, what result it helps with, what is not included, and how to buy: [paste offer]."

Prompt:

"Create a simple comparison table for these three packages: [package details]. Help customers choose without making exaggerated claims."

A clearer offer can improve conversion without changing your product.

## Social media and content prompts

Prompt:

"Create 20 educational content ideas for a [business type] serving [audience]. Use real customer questions, objections, mistakes, and buying concerns. Avoid generic motivational content."

Prompt:

"Turn this customer question into a helpful post. Explain the issue, common mistake, practical advice, and a soft call to action: [question]."

Prompt:

"Create a 4-week content plan for my business. Include educational posts, proof posts, FAQ posts, and offer posts. My main service is [service]."

If you want broader AI productivity prompts, read [Best ChatGPT Prompts for Everyday Work](/blog/best-chatgpt-prompts-for-everyday-work/).

## Proposal prompts

Prompt:

"Create a proposal outline for a client who needs [problem solved]. Include problem summary, proposed solution, deliverables, timeline, assumptions, client responsibilities, and next step."

Prompt:

"Rewrite this proposal to make it clearer and more persuasive without sounding exaggerated: [paste proposal]."

Prompt:

"List the risks, unclear points, and missing information in this proposal before I send it: [paste proposal]."

The last prompt is important. AI should help you catch gaps, not only write polished sentences.

## SOP and team prompts

Prompt:

"Turn these instructions into a step-by-step SOP for my team. Use simple language. Include checklist, quality standard, common mistakes, and escalation rules: [notes]."

Prompt:

"Create a daily checklist for a [role] in a [business type]. Include opening tasks, customer tasks, admin tasks, reporting, and closing tasks."

Prompt:

"Rewrite this staff instruction so it is clearer and easier to follow: [instruction]."

Good SOPs reduce repeated questions and protect the owner from becoming the only person who knows how things work.

## Hiring and delegation prompts

Prompt:

"Write a role description for [role]. Include responsibilities, required skills, performance expectations, tools used, and interview questions."

Prompt:

"Create an interview scorecard for hiring [role]. Include criteria, what a strong answer looks like, and red flags."

Prompt:

"Help me decide which tasks to delegate first. Here is what I do every week: [list tasks]. Group them by high judgement, repeatable, admin, and revenue-related tasks."

Delegation becomes easier when work is documented clearly.

## Analysis prompts

Prompt:

"Analyze these customer complaints and group them by root cause. Suggest process fixes, communication fixes, and training needs: [paste complaints]."

Prompt:

"Review these sales notes and identify common objections, buying signals, and follow-up opportunities: [paste notes]."

Prompt:

"Here are this month's business numbers: [numbers]. Ask me clarifying questions, then identify what I should investigate first."

Do not paste sensitive data unless you are comfortable with the tool and your privacy settings. Remove names, emails, phone numbers, and payment details where possible.

## Follow-up prompts

Prompt:

"Write a polite follow-up message for someone who asked about [offer] but has not replied in [number] days. Keep it helpful and include one clear next step."

Prompt:

"Create a 5-message follow-up sequence for a warm lead. The goal is to educate, answer objections, and invite them to take action without pressure."

Prompt:

"Rewrite this follow-up so it sounds less desperate and more useful: [message]."

Many businesses do not need more leads first. They need better follow-up.

## Save your best prompts

Create a simple document called "Business AI Prompts" and save prompts that consistently work. Organize them by customer service, content, sales, SOPs, proposals, and analysis.

Over time, this becomes a business asset.

## FAQ

**Can business owners use ChatGPT without being technical?**  
Yes. Most business use cases involve communication, planning, and organization.

**Should I let staff use these prompts?**  
Yes, but give them rules about customer data, review, and what decisions still need approval.

**What is the best first prompt to use?**  
Start by asking AI to list your repeated weekly tasks and identify which can become templates.
